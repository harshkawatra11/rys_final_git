About the Conference

First of Its Kind in East Uttar Pradesh

Gorakhpur Yuva Samvad was successfully held on the 11th and 12th of October 2025 at H.P. Children's Academy, Gorakhpur, in association with Rajdhani Yuva Sansad. The two-day event witnessed the participation of over 900 enthusiastic students across 19 dynamic committees.

The conference was the first of its kind in East Uttar Pradesh, and its inception is credited to the initial concept proposed by Mr. Atirek Shahi. Students from Classes 4 to 12 engaged in parliamentary, diplomatic, and policy simulations with months of dedicated preparation — starting from ice-breaking sessions in July through full mock setups the week before the conference.

Key Highlights

What Made This Event Special

  • First-ever youth parliamentary conference in East UP
  • 900+ students across 19 diverse committees covering Lok Sabha, UNGA, UNHRC, NITI Aayog, NCW, and more
  • Multi-month preparation: July ice-breakers, August-September training, online doubt-clearing sessions
  • Participants from Classes 4 to 12 — junior and senior-level committees
  • International Press committee with Journalists, Photojournalists, Vloggers, Cartoonists, and Memers
  • Felicitation across Best Speaker, High Commendation, Special Mention, and Honourable Mention categories

19 Committees

A Wide Range of Forums

LS
Lok Sabha (Junior)
Stray Animals & Peaceful Co-existence

Ruling side proposed animal care centres and community feeding programmes; opposition highlighted implementation capacity and monitoring.

UN
United Nations
Achieving the Sustainable Development Goals

Delegates proposed a global sustainability fund, technology transfer for developing countries, and green innovation — highlighting challenges of inequality and the digital divide.

WHO
World Health Organisation
Promoting Child Health, Hygiene, and Nutrition

Comprehensive framework for preventive healthcare, community health awareness, and integrated nutrition programmes for underprivileged regions.

RS
Rajya Sabha
Affordable and Accessible Healthcare for All

Proposed primary healthcare centres in rural areas, telemedicine services, and expanded medical insurance coverage for low-income families.

UNGA
UNGA
Humanitarian Crisis in Gaza

Emphasised coordinated international humanitarian assistance and discussed accountability for human rights violations under international law.

UP
UP Legislative Assembly
Digital Governance & E-Literacy

Discussed transformative digital governance through unified service portals, mobile governance apps, and targeted digital literacy programmes.
