About the Conference

Where It All Began

Sajag Yuva Manch 1.0 was the inaugural edition of what would become Rajdhani Yuva Sansad's most iconic annual conference series. Held in 2019 at Silverline Prestige School, Ghaziabad, this founding event brought together approximately 250 students across five diverse committees.

The success of SYM 1.0 established a proven model for multi-committee parliamentary and diplomatic simulation that would grow year-on-year, eventually scaling to 700+ participants by the 5th edition. This inaugural event marked RYS's journey from a local school initiative to a nationally recognised youth parliament organisation.

Key Highlights

What Made This Event Special

  • Inaugural launch of what became RYS's signature annual series
  • ~250 students from schools in the NCR region
  • 5 committees: Lok Sabha, UNGA, All-India Political Parties Meet (AIPPM), Historical Crisis (Partition of India, 1947), and International Press
  • First ever RYS multi-committee conference in collaboration with Silverline Prestige School
  • Established the SYM format that would grow to 700+ participants by 2024
  • Proof-of-concept for RYS's model of accessible, high-quality parliamentary simulation
5 Committees

The Original Lineup

LS
Lok Sabha
National Legislative Debate

Students simulated the floor of India's Lower House, debating pressing national legislation in a formal parliamentary setting.

UNGA
United Nations General Assembly
Global Issues in Focus

Delegates represented UN member nations, deliberating on international challenges including climate, security, and development.

AIPPM
All-India Political Parties Meet
Cross-Party Dialogue

Students represented different Indian political parties in a cross-party negotiation format, simulating coalition and consensus-building.

HSM
Historical Crisis
The Partition of India, 1947

Re-examination of one of history's most transformative and tragic events through historical simulation and empathetic deliberation.

IP
International Press
Reporting the Conference

Student journalists covered the conference in real time, developing skills in journalism, media ethics, and public communication.
